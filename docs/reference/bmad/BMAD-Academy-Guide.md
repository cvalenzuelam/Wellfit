🤔 Okay But... What Even IS BMAD?

BMAD stands for Build Me A Dream. And yes, it lives up to the name.

At its heart, BMAD is a methodology — a smart, structured way of using AI agents to take your team from a half-baked idea scribbled on a sticky note all the way to production-ready, tested, shipped software. Without losing your mind in the process.

Here's the fun part: it's not just one AI. It's a whole crew of specialized agents, each with their own lane:

💻 So What is BMAD Chat?

Think of BMAD Chat as the front door to the whole BMAD world. It's a web app where every role on your team — PO, Dev, QA, Scrum Master — gets a guided, structured experience without needing to be an AI whisperer or a command-line ninja.

Here's what's waiting for you inside:

🗨️ Claude Direct Chat

Your main interface for talking to the agents. Pick an agent, describe what you're working on, and let it guide you. It's conversational, intuitive, and surprisingly fun.

🖥️ PTY Terminal

For the developers who want to get their hands dirty. Full terminal access for deeper control. Power users, this one's for you.

📊 Feature Workflow Dashboard

A visual command center showing every feature your team is working on — where it is in the lifecycle, what files exist, what's been created. No more "wait, where did we land on that feature?" conversations.

🗂️ Multi-Product Workspaces

Working on more than one product? No problem. BMAD Chat supports multiple workspaces, each wired up to its own Git repo (GitHub or Azure DevOps). Everything stays organized and separate.

🔐 Oh, and Security?

You log in with your company Azure AD account — same as your email and Microsoft 365. No new passwords, no new accounts, no sticky notes on your monitor. Sessions are secure, isolated, and sandboxed. Your IT team already loves this.

From sticky-note idea to live feature. With every role supported the whole way. Pretty neat, right?

🎫 BMAD + JIRA — Better Together

JIRA and BMAD are best friends. Here's how they talk to each other:

BMAD → JIRA (the easy part)

When the PM agent finishes generating epics and stories, they automatically push into JIRA as tickets. Your team sees them on the board and starts picking them up. Zero copy-paste. Zero "can you format that as a story?" emails.

JIRA → BMAD (the clever part)

QA engineers log bugs in JIRA the way they always have. A webhook then automatically pulls those bugs back into BMAD so developers can action them without leaving their workflow. Bugs in JIRA? Dev sees them in BMAD. Seamless.

⚠️ Heads up: Status updates are manual for now. This is intentional — we're building trust with AI-assisted workflows one step at a time. As confidence grows, so will the automation. Baby steps. 👶

The Golden Rule 🥇

Nothing — and we mean NOTHING — goes to production without UAT sign-off from Product.

Rob is the keeper of the release. He's the one who gets sign-off and pushes to prod. If you're not Rob, you don't release to prod. If you are Rob — no pressure. 😅

🚫 What BMAD is NOT

Let's bust some myths before they become bad habits:

❌ "BMAD will just do everything for me" Nope! Agents produce great drafts. YOU make the final call. Always. The human is always in the loop.

❌ "I can just auto-deploy from BMAD" Hard no. Every change still goes through your normal review and release process.

❌ "It's just a fancy chatbot" It's a structured methodology. The agents have specific purposes, produce specific outputs, and follow a specific process. Treat them like a specialist, not Google.

❌ "Once I set it up, I never have to think again" After a long session, AI can start to lose the plot a little — responses feel off, context gets fuzzy. It's normal. Just start a fresh session and you're golden. ✨
